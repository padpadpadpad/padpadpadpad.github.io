The building of a custom bird feeder

Mon, Nov 18, 2019 birds, soph, personal

Me and Soph, my partner of 8 years, have never found particular enjoyment in the birds around us. Sure I loved seeing a robin, who doesn’t, and Soph has always loved ducks, but the other birds in the garden barely registered.

However, 2019 is the year we became garden bird aware. Unfortunately, 2019 is also the worst year of our lives. Soph’s health has deteriorated further. She is essentially bed bound with constant extreme, excrutiating, indescribale pain all over her body. It is so painful her speech is impaired. She has constant vertigo and only gets one hour’s sleep a night. She is completely dependent her parents (who she lives with) and me when I visit. I juggle a post-doc in Cornwall and coming up every other week for a long weekend. There is currently no respite on the horizon, with the local NHS trust not knowing what do with her we are kind of left to cope with it ourselves while also working out what our next move is. It is, to say the least, challenging. I have no idea how she does it everyday.

Soph looks out into the garden once a day while she is having breakfast. We have made a bird feeder that can be seen from upstairs. In those thirty minutes, we sit together watching the birds that come visit. It is her only view into the outside world. Early on in the year we bought a feeder from a garden centre and loads of food from HappyBeaks. When she moved upstairs her dad strapped a stick to the top of pole and we positioned one feeder every day at the top to bring the birds in. Then in mid-summer me and John made the behemoth bird feeder, that we can raise and lower everyday. It allows her to have a perfect view of 3 bird feeders every morning.

She loves the birds. Her favourite is the robin, but it prefers foraging lower down on the window feeder. That just makes it especially special when she does see it. She has loved watching goldfinch fledglings gain their beautiful, red strike. We’ve both smiled as the goldfinch has flocked

For me, I find it very therapeutic.
